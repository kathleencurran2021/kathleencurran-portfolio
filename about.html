Great to meet you!

I always loved solving puzzles growing up. Mix in a fascination for technology, and software engineering seemed like a natural career path for me.

While full of challenging puzzles, it lacked a direct connection with the people I was trying to help. I used to spend ages trying to determine how I could build something in a way that would be easy for people to use. But as hard as I tried to make my developer job more user-focused, that simply isn’t the role.

After some deep reflection, I decided to switch careers into UX, where I could take on a much more user-focused role.

I have always been passionate about designing technology for users, but this time it’s actually in my job description.

I’m currently completing my degree in UX and HCI at the University of Michigan.

I am in UX because I aim to improve the lives of users and ensure their needs are being met.

My approach to UX is both relationship-oriented and data-informed . Taking a relationship-oriented approach to UX prioritizes empathy for users, while data-informed approach to UX helps ensure my work stays relevant by quantifying the impact the changes will have and drives further innovation.
