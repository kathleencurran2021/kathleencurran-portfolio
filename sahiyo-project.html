Sahiyo: Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation

Project Specs

  • Time Frame: February 2022
  • Role: UX Researcher
  • Team: Austin Williams, Grace Brindle, and
    Alyssa Downs

Skills

  • Usability Testing
  • Semi-Structured Interviewing
  • Qualitative and Quantitative Data Analysis
  • Heuristic Evaluation

Overview

Sahiyo (pronounced Say-yo) is an international organization dedicated to fighting for women's rights, specifically in the area of female genital cutting (FGC). They work to empower communities to end FGC and create social change through education and community involvement on their website, sahiyo.org.




The Challenge

Sahiyo uses their website to guide community involvement. The organization posts resources for victims or people who want to help, testimonies from people involved in FGC, and information about community events. However, the website is not always accessible to those with disabilities, and users often have difficulty finding the resources they need, particularly with event information. We then created the following statement to guide our research:

“What challenges do users encounter when they use Sahiyo’s website, and how will solving those issues increase site-wide accessibility and user engagement?”

Scope and Constraints

The project was limited to four months, which made it difficult to spend as much time as we would have liked in data collection. Due to this time constraint, we were only able to work with the desktop version of sahiyo.org, even though there is a mobile version of the site. Additionally, it was difficult to recruit participants for interviews, a survey, and user testing, in part because we were unable to provide a monetary incentive. We recruited mostly from Sahiyo’s contacts and word of mouth from our team.

Process

Interview Study

We conducted an interview study which sought to understand the primary goals, pain points, and needs of users navigating the Sahiyo website.

We conducted six interviews with users and stakeholders on Zoom, each one lasting between 30-60 minutes. Interviewees were recruited through Sahiyo leadership and through online forums for people with disabilities.

We found that:
  1. Users believed that sahiyo.org was mostly effective at meeting their needs
  2. Users struggled with accessibility issues like responsiveness, especially with the mobile version
  3. Many users believed that storytelling (such as the blog) is a vital part of connecting the Sahiyo community
  4. Users often accidentally navigated to Sahiyo’s old website (sahiyo.com), since it is more prevalent in Google search results

Personas

We used information from our interviews to create personas for sahiyo.org users. These personas allowed us to better understand our target audience and keep in mind user's needs as we made decisions.


Survey

To better understand how users of varying abilities engaged with sahiyo.org, we launched a survey to the Sahiyo community, asking about their experience with the website, their role in Sahiyo, their disability status, and how often they used the site.

The survey had several limitations that weakened our data’s accuracy. First, we did not receive enough responses for our survey results to be statistically significant. Secondly, the majority of respondents were either Sahiyo staff or volunteers which may not be representative of typical users.

However, our results indicated that users with disabilities had a more difficult time using the site than those without disabilities.

While the survey gathered general data about users’ experiences on the website, it did not provide any details behind why users felt the way they did. Because of this, we decided to perform a heuristic evaluation to find specific instances of usability issues, and usability testing to get further insight on our survey responses.

Heuristic Evaluation

Because of our results from the survey, we wanted to determine the specific accessibility issues with sahiyo.org. Each team member examined the website according to Nielsen's usability heuristics. We prioritized each issue by severity level.

Most of the issues we found were related to the visual design of the site. We recommended that Sahiyo implement several changes to the website, such as increasing the color contrast of fonts and buttons, adding alternative text to images, increasing keyboard accessibility, and adding text decoration to links to make them more distinct.

Usability Testing

In our usability tests, we wanted to assess users' ability to find information on sahiyo.org, specifically those who have visual impairments such as low vision or colorblindness.

We used word of mouth, online forums, and survey responses to recruit users with visual disabilities and/or Sahiyo connections. We recruited five individuals of varying ages, disabilities, and levels of experience with sahiyo.org.

Tests were conducted via Zoom and ranged from 20 – 60 minutes. View Protocol Here Users generally had high success with general navigation within the site. Users experienced the most difficulty locating an event in the calendar which had the lowest average satisfaction rate at 1.6 and the lowest success rate at 50%. Additionally, some aspects of the site were out of date or broken, leaving users frustrated.

For each task, the we recorded the participant's completion of the task, the time they took, and how satisfying they found the task. We also kept observations of participants in a color-coded spreadsheet, which allowed us to keep track of common issues and comments that users encountered.
