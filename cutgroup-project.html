Civic User Testing

Improving the Michigan Poll Work Experience
  • Role: UX Researcher
  • Client: Michigan Bureau of Elections
  • Skills: Rapid User Testing
  • Tools: Miro, Figma
  • Team: Grace Brindle, Emilee Kutyla, Rebecca Xu
  • Duration: February 2022

Problem
The Michigan Bureau of Elections provides a dense paper manual at all polling locations to help poll workers with any issues that arise at the polls. As they look towards future digitization, they aren’t sure how poll workers use the manual, if at all.

Solution
We reorganized the manual based on what different poll workers would need, and created a visual hierarchy guide to increase searchability in the dense manual.

Overview

In a time when elections are fraught with anxiety, the Michigan Bureau of Elections (BOE) ensures that all Michigan elections are safe, organized, and legal. A state-run entity, the BOE helps local election officials on Election Day by providing them with the support and resources to make the election run smoothly.

The Challenge

Our challenge was to improve the BOE’s Election Inspector's Procedure Manual, a dense paper manual that the BOE supplies to each election location on Election Day as a resource to poll workers. While thorough, the 40-page manual was dense and lacked searchability, particularly within the chaos that can arise in a busy polling location.

Our goals were as follows:
  1. Understand poll workers' current experiences with the manual.
  2. Test the manual with local poll workers to determine both degree and ease of use.
  3. Make specific recommendations that would allow poll workers to more easily solve any issues that arise on Election Day.

Scope and Constraints

Civic User Testing Group (CUTGroup) projects are designed to be short and fast-paced in order to make public applications more accessible and useful to residents. One of the main constraints was the three-week time frame, which meant that we were limited on how deep our research could go or how particular we could be about recruiting participants.

Rapid Testing, Rapid Results

In this project, gaining quick and actionable insights was vital. Conducting rapid user testing allowed us to quickly understand user experience and identify pain points with the manual. We were well aware of the flaws and understand that rapid testing doesn't offer the same breadth or depth of insights, the quick turnaround time made it the perfect method for this project.

Testing the Manual

After meeting with our BOE stakeholder, we came up with a protocol and series of tasks for our users to test. These tests required them to flip through a copy of the paper Election Inspector’s Manual (provided by us).

Our users were recruited by both our BOE representative and through contacts within the University of Michigan. We ended up with 10 users total, all of whom had worked the polls in the state of Michigan. Our users had varying levels of experience, ages, locations they had worked the polls, and political affiliations.

The user testing took place over one evening, with each interview lasting around 30 minutes.

Key Findings
  1. The manual's current structure does not align with the user's expectations
  2. Poll workers prefer easy-to-read instructions, but the manual is dense
  3. Visual hierarchy and graphics makes content easier to find
  4. The manual may serve different purposes depending on the user's role at the polling location

Recommendations

1. Restructure the manual to be in chronological order

Many users expected the manual to be in chronological order, or similar topics to be in the same location and had difficulty finding the right information.

Reordering the manual to be in chronological order would better align the manual with user assumptions.

I thought it would be chronological for the day, so I would assume [closing the polls] is at the back
2. Include eye-catching visuals that highlight important information and improve visual hierarchy

Users noted that things like bolded text, headers, and graphics helped them find and understand key information.

3. Divide the manual into two parts based on roles.

Typical poll workers tend to use the manual only for procedural tasks, and then ask the precinct chair whenever issues arise. Because of this, we suggest splitting the manual into two parts.

Part 1 would include step by step procedures for all poll workers to follow, such as setting up the precinct or closing the polls.

Part 2 would include more detailed information about edge cases or issues that might arise on Election day. This part is intended for the precinct chair, who generally has more authority and experience to solve problems at the polls.

4. Create quick reference guides for poll workers and voters.

Because the manual has so much information, it is difficult to find the right information quickly.

We propose a series of ‘Voters, Know Your Rights’ posters that can be displayed around the precinct to help guide voters at the polls to give voters easy access to important information, instead of having to rely on poll workers.

We also propose creating infographic outlines to common procedures and issues that poll workers can keep on the table with them to quickly reference in the case of a question to reduce the time spent searching in the manual.

Conclusion

In the end, the Michigan Bureau of Elections included over 60% of the team's recommendations into the new version of the Election Inspector's Procedure Manual, including updating the Table of Contents, adding bullet points and numbering for procedural tasks, and putting relevant tasks into chronological order.

While our methods and findings certainly were not perfect, we did make a positive impact on the Michigan BOE by giving them the data they needed to make informed, user-centric decisions about how to update the manual for the upcoming 2022 midterm elections.

What I Learned

While a lot of UX research tends to focus on digital tools like websites, it still has great uses for things that are not on the web. CUTgroup was an excellent practice in applying UX research methods to solve offline problems .

One of the big themes of CUTGroup is, 'Don't let perfect be the enemy of the good.' In a project like this with such a short timeline, we simply didn't have time to make things perfect. It was tempting to think in terms of ‘if we only had more time we could…’ but instead we focused on the areas we could add the most value.
